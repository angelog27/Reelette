# Product

## Register
product

## Users
Multi-service subscribers overwhelmed by the paradox of choice — 3-4 subscriptions,
20,000+ titles, still nothing to watch. They use the app in the evening, solo or
coordinating with friends for a group watch. Core need: stop scrolling, commit to
something good, fast.

## Product Purpose
Reelette ends decision paralysis for streaming subscribers. The flagship feature is
the Reelette wheel — a roulette mechanic that spins to find your movie based on your
filters (genre, mood, provider, runtime, rating), turning the choice into something
exciting rather than exhausting. It connects all your services in one place so you
see what you can actually watch right now.

Core surfaces:
- Discover — service buttons (Netflix, Disney+, Hulu, etc.) that open each provider's
  catalog; shows what's available to watch and what you've already watched from that
  service; each movie card carries its streaming service logo in the corner
- Reelette wheel — the signature spin mechanic; genre chips shift the entire
  color theme of the wheel
- My Stuff — personal space with three tabs:
  - Watch Later — saved movies to watch in the future
  - Watched — movies marked as seen, each with a 1–10 personal rating
  - Stats — derived from watch history: top 10 rated films, most frequent actors
    and directors, genre breakdown, most watched studio, decade breakdown, most
    watched platform, and a taste profile with derived tags and rewatch rate
- Movie Groups — private groups with invite codes, real-time chat, and
  collaborative rankings where each member's score feeds a group aggregate;
  "Together" mode pools everyone's services to find what the whole group can
  watch, "Away" mode matches two people across their own libraries
- Community feed — public social layer (Twitter for what you're watching);
  friend management, watchlist sharing, notifications

Success: from "I don't know what to watch" to watching something in under 2 minutes.

## Brand Personality
Confident, Cinematic, Social. Sleek and put-together — the feel of a real theatre:
anticipation, occasion, quality. Not cluttered. Not cartoon. Not aimed at kids.
A film-literate friend who always knows what to watch, not an algorithm.
Emotional goals: the excitement of the spin, the pleasure of a great pick,
the warmth of sharing taste with people you trust.

## Anti-references
- Netflix clone: red-on-black, identical card grids, no personality
- Cheap streaming aggregator: cluttered, ad-heavy, 2015 web energy
- Soulless SaaS: generic clean — fintech dashboard with content swapped in
- Kid-friendly / cartoon UI: bubbly corners, bright primaries, playful icons
- IMDB's current design: data-heavy without soul, no sense of occasion

## Design Principles
1. Cinema, not catalogue — screens carry the weight and anticipation of a theatre
   lobby. Refined, not flashy.
2. The spin is the hero — the Reelette wheel is the product's identity. Every
   design decision should make that moment feel special.
3. Social depth, not social noise — the feed and groups enrich discovery; they
   never compete with it. Friends' taste is signal.
4. Decisive by design — every interaction moves the user toward a choice.
   Indecision is the enemy; the pick feels inevitable and fun.
5. Earned personality — character through craft: typography weight, intentional
   spacing, texture. Not decoration for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA baseline. Reduced motion support (prefers-reduced-motion). Sufficient
contrast in dark and light modes. Icon-only controls must have accessible labels.
